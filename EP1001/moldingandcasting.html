What is molding and casting

You would have to design a mold and cut it out of blue form. You can cast your mold using any any viscous liquid that would harden to form the final product. Watch the video above to see an example of how it's done.

Preparing My mold

Step 1

Prepare a model of the item you would like to cast. I am not about to teach you how I created mine so just download if you like it. Take note that you would be using a 2D CNC to machine your model out, so remember to not have any undercut. My lecturer had specified that our work peice can only be of 2 thicknesses of foam - 25mm & 50 mm. Give allowance if at least 4-5 mm for base. 100mm in length max.

Step 2

Encase your model in a box. Ensure that the box is a new body and that the model is fully encased.

Step 3

Using the combine tool, select the box as the target and the model you want to cast as tool body. It should look like the right photo.

Step 4

Now encase your box with model cut-out into a bigger box. It is important you name your bodies to ensure you do not get confused.

Step 5

Repeat what you did for step 3. This part would be CNC out to help make a silicon mold, you would understand more at the bottom.

Making My Mold

Start by cutting out the appropriate size of foam you would need for your mold and secure the foam to the machine via double sided tape.

Put in your tool and have the height celebrated to the foam. If you plan to use 2 different tools, ensure there is an area for you to celebrate the height again.

This is a long process and it's recommanded you have book with you as you wait for this to finish.

This is how it look like after the CNC.

Issues faced during CNC

Limited by tool's length, I had to make pauses and shave off a few layers of the foam to prevent the Multi Chuck from touching the foam.

Making the silicon mold

Please prepare all necessary PPE and follow all safety precautions before following this steps.

Step 1: Pour part A and B in separate cups to ensure there is no mix up. The ratio is 1:1
Step 2: Set a timer for 15 mins. This is the amount of time you have before the mixture starts hardening after contact.
 Step 3: Mix both part A and B together and mixing slowly to prevert any air bubbles from forming.
Step 4: From a corner of the cnc mold, pour the mixture slowly ensuring everything is covered in silicon.

After pouring, allow silicon to dry for at least 24 hour. (48h to be safe)

Check if the silicon is dry by lifting an area you are okay with losing. Once dry, the silicon mold can be removed from the foam

Unless you have coated the cnc mold with a layer of mold removal, there is no way you can remove the silicon without distroying the cnc mold.

Clean up the mold by removing any overspill using a penknife. Your mold is ready to be used now.

Catocolate

Start by getting a bar of chocolate. The value store is selling theirs for SGD2.50(currently 2023).
It is recommanded to get Dark or Milk chocolate.

Cut up the chocolate into thin strips. The thinner the chocolate, the easier it is for it to melt.
Keep in mind that any water would degrade the chocolate.

The old method of melting the chocolate is too time consuming(anything >15mins) for melting chocolate.

Hence, I asked chef mic for help and got the job done in 30 secs.
I found that mixing the chocolate every 10 sec improves the quality. The end result should feel like a soft playdoh.

Using a knife or something with a flat surface, pack the chocolate into the silicon mold ensuring the mold is fill of chocolate.

Place the chocolate filled mold into the fridge. I placed mine in the freezer because it's easier to remove it from the mold later.

After the chocolate is cooled, you can remove the catocolate from the mold.

As the mold is undamaged after use, the same catocolate can be made over and over again.
