Important features

It is important to "dogbone" connecting areas in your model and ensure there are no sharp corners as the CNC is unable to cut it out.
Adding chamfers would allow your parts to be fitted easier.

Stress testing

This process is done to ensure your model is strong enough to hold what ever you are placing on it. As listed in my requirements,
I would like my flower rack to hold a min of 10kg and 15kg max. The photo above demonstrate and shows you the flaws in any design.
Without further ado, start by switching to "simulation"


Constrains

Constrain acts as the anchor point in which your part would be resting upon.
(It means the area your model touching the floor when you place it down)

Selecting material

As ply-wood is not a orthotropic material, fusion 360 is unable to simulate it.
So change the material to MDF where the structures are similar.

Load area

Now selecting load, click on the area where your model would experience load, fusion uses "Newton" so multiply your load with "9.81".

This is how it should look once you are done.

Pre-checks

Click on "pre-check" would tell you what other errors you might face and changes you need to do before performing the stress check.

final checks

Upon clicking results, fusion would warn you about any fault your model have. click solve once all faults are repaired.

Stress Test Results

Once the simulation is complete, You are able to see what part of the model is experiencing the most stress.
Increasing the deformation would allow you to have a clearer picture of any deformation occuring.

DXF placement

Align your DXF files so you do not waste material(even when my model require 2 big ply wood).
